        Objective Part 2: Technical Entrepreneur Part 2 further explored the fundamentals of technology entrepreneurship.

        Taken From: A Day in the Life of an Entrepreneur

          • We learned the process technology entrepreneurs use to take a validated idea to the larger market and scale their startup.

          • The class demonstrated the entrepreneurial mindset: where others see insurmountable problems, entrepreneurs look for opportunities in technology and business solutions.

          • An entrepreneurial perspective is also a way of thinking in order to tackle new opportunities in social entrepreneurship, whether it is in government or NGOs (non government organizations).

          • Part 2 of this class covers an opportunity execution project (OEP), where you test the rest of the aspects of the business model and figure out how to actually commercialize the product or service.
          • In Part 2 of Technical Entrepreneur, my partner from Part 1 with whom we did the Fiction Recommender Web Site, did not participate in Part 2, so instead, I joined the Pass Port Social Web Site Team, with Will Bruner whom asked me to join his team and later a side venture start up in wearable computing, although I never did end up joining the real life venture he was investigating at this time.

          • Opportunity Execution Project (OEP): The Passport Social Website was similar in many ways to FaceBook, except geared toward the travel industry, with different levels of membership, where you could follow friends as they traveled and be directed to a travel web site if you were interested in a similar vacation.
